My Background

When I went back to school at the start of the pandemic I was pursuing a paralegal degree. However, I quickly realized that programming was my real passion. I bought The Self-Taught Prgrammer 1st edition by Cory Althoff and also, A Beginner's Guide to JavaScript 4th Edition, by John Pollack. I was hooked. So I soon enrolled in the University of Louisville's College of Business, pursuing a Computer Information Systems Degree with a Web Development Track.

It has been a challenging and rewarding experience. I have learned so much beyond coding and business theory. Such as developing inter-personal skills by being a member of 3 different project groups, and how to manage time between school and work.

When I first enrolled into the College of Business as a CIS major, I had only studied Python and Javascript. Since then I have learned and accomplished so much more, including:

  • Studied many programming languages in class and in my free time, including: HTML5, CSS, XML, JavaScript, JQuery, Bootstrap, JSON, Ajax, PHP, SQL, MySQL, C#, C++, C, Python
  • Built over 40 programs and websites.
  • Was a Lab Assistant for a C# class. I was responsible for designing 4 program assignments, coming up with exam questions, and grading 4 programs and 3 exams.
  • I worked at the REACH Center, tutoring students in C#, HTML, CSS, Access, and Excel.
  • I achieved a College Reading and Learing Association Level II tutor Certificate.

I love to learn, and spend a lot of my free time reading all sorts of programming books! To the left you'll see my current stack of books I plan to read or have partially read.

In the future I hope to be a part of an organization that has a passion for IT and for giving back to the community by sharing the IT knowledge we have gained through the years.
